  • Taj Mahal: A Monument of Eternal Love

    Located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, the Taj Mahal is a stunning white marble mausoleum built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who passed away during childbirth. The Taj Mahal is renowned for its magnificent architecture, intricate carvings, and symmetrical gardens, making it one of the most beautiful and iconic landmarks in the world. As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it stands as a symbol of love, devotion, and architectural brilliance, attracting millions of visitors every year.

  • Gateway of India: The Majestic Archway

    Located in Mumbai, the Gateway of India is a monumental archway built in 1911 to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India. Standing at 26 meters tall, this iconic structure combines Hindu and Muslim architectural styles, with intricate carvings and stunning details. Overlooking the Arabian Sea, it has witnessed significant events in India's history, including the departure of the last British troops from India in 1948. Today, it is a popular tourist attraction and a symbol of India's rich colonial and cultural heritage.

  • Sun Temple: The Chariot of the Sun God

    Located in Konark, Odisha, the Sun Temple is an architectural marvel built in the 13th century. Shaped like a chariot with 24 intricately carved wheels, it is dedicated to the Sun God, Surya. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a stunning example of ancient Indian craftsmanship.
